LiBackpack DGC50 is a backpack-based LiDAR 3D scanning system that is suitable for scanning both indoor and outdoor environments. It possesses exceptional absolute geographic coordinate capabilities. By utilizing GNSS information and SLAM technology, LiBackpack DGC50 can capture point cloud data with precise absolute coordinates and panoramic images simultaneously. Even in areas where the GNSS signal is weak along the scanning route, it is still able to obtain geographic coordinate information. This equipment has extensive applications in various fields such as integrated measurement for indoor and outdoor spaces, acquiring information in underground areas, tunnel engineering, digital factories, digital mines, forestry resource census, BIM, and other related industries.

Data collection

  • Open the battery compartment covers and insert charged batteries on both sides.
    (?)
  • Turn on the batteries by pressing their buttons once, and then press and hold for 2 seconds. The red light on the button of the batteries will illuminate, indicating they have been successfully turned on.
    (?)
  • Wait for 1-2 minutes after the system has been launched to enable all functions to start.
    (?)
  • Check the back part of the system to ensure that the three LED lights are solid green.
    (?)
  • Open the Wi-Fi settings of your tablet/phone and select the network named "LiBackpack" from the list of available networks.
    (?)
  • When prompted, enter the default password :"greenvalley".
    (?)
  • Launch Google Chrome (or any other browser) and navigate to 192.168.12.1 to access the LiBackpack User Interface. If unsuccessful, double-check that the Wi-Fi is connected to LiBackpack. Move to an open area away from tall buildings.
    (?)
  • Wait for the GPS status to change to "Locked" state.
    (?)
  • Ensure that the system is connected to at least 20 satellites (SatNum is not less than 20 in the menu). If not, move to more open areas.
    (?)
  • Tap on the "Start" button located in the lower-left corner of the browser-based LiBackpack user interface. Wait until additional statistics are displayed on the screen, indicating that the system is running properly.
    (?)
  • Start walking in an "8"-shaped pattern for approximately 1 minute for calibration purposes.
    (?)
  • Once you walk your path, move into an open area where the satellite number is more than 20. It doesn't have to be the starting point.
    (?)
  • Stand still for 3-5 minutes.
    (?)
  • Press the Stop Button located in the bottom left corner of the web-based UI.
    (?)
  • Wait for the countdown window to close and the current collection session to be completed.
    (?)
