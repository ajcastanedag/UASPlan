Micasense Altum

The Micasens Altum is a multispectral camera that captures images across multiple wavelength bands, which can be used for various applications such as precision agriculture, mineral exploration, and environmental monitoring. The camera typically uses multiple image sensors to capture data in each band, such as the visible, near-infrared, or short-wave infrared regions. The Altum camera is designed to be mounted on a drone or other platform for aerial imaging. However, it can also be used as a stationary device for ground-based imaging. The camera can generate detailed information about the reflective properties of the target, enabling applications such as crop health analysis, mineral mapping, and object recognition. It can also be used to measure temperature and thermal infrared imaging. The Altum camera has a high-resolution sensor and advanced optics to produce high-quality images. The camera typically outputs image data in various formats, such as TIFF or JPEG, and may also include additional data, such as GPS coordinates and altitude information. Additionally, the Altum can be integrated with other sensors, such as LiDAR, allowing for a more comprehensive data collection and analysis.

The Micasense Downwelling Light Sensor 2 (DLS 2) is a sensor that works with the multispectral camera and must be included in the setup to measure the light condition changes during the data acquisition. This sensor measures the amount of light that is downwelling or coming down from the sky in a given area. It is typically used to measure the amount of light available for photosynthesis in crops, which can be used to analyze crop health and predict crop yields. The DLS 2 sensor is designed to be mounted on a drone or other platform for aerial measurements, and it can also be used as a stationary device for ground-based measurements. The sensor has a wide field of view, allowing it to measure the light over a large area and is equipped with advanced optics to produce accurate and reliable measurements. The sensor typically outputs data in various formats, such as CSV or JSON, and may include additional data, such as GPS coordinates and altitude information. The DLS 2 sensor can be used with other sensors, such as multispectral cameras, to capture a more comprehensive data set and extract valuable insights.
